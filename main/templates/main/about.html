About Fergusson College Pune

Fergusson College Pune is one of the oldest and most prestigious colleges in India. It was founded in 1885 by Deccan Education Society, and is named after Sir James Fergusson, Governor of Bombay at the time. The college offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in various fields such as arts, science, and commerce.

Over the years, Fergusson College has produced many notable alumni, including social reformer Gopal Krishna Gokhale, freedom fighter Bal Gangadhar Tilak, and scientist C.V. Raman. The college has also been associated with several social and political movements in India, and has a rich history of academic excellence.

Today, Fergusson College is recognized as one of the top colleges in India, and continues to attract students from all over the country.
